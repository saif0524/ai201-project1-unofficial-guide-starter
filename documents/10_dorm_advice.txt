OP: Moving into the residence halls in August. What do I actually need to know?

comment:
Living on campus is the easiest way to skip the parking/commute problem entirely. You're a
walk from class and the shuttle hits the halls. Don't bring a car your first year if you can
help it.

comment:
Bring a fan and a mattress topper. The rooms run warm early in the semester and the standard
mattresses are rough. A shower caddy and shower shoes if you're on a shared bathroom floor.

comment:
Get to know your RA. They run the floor events (free food, again) and they're your first stop
when something breaks or roommate stuff gets weird.

comment:
The dining hall is closest to the halls so you'll eat there a lot — see the meal swipe advice
everyone gives, ration them. The on-campus markets take dining dollars for late-night snacks.

comment:
Lock your door. Sounds obvious. Campus is safe but stuff walks off in dorms when doors are
propped.

comment:
Move-in day is chaos. Sign up for an early time slot, bring a rolling cart or a friend with
strong arms, and label your boxes. The elevators get backed up for hours.

comment:
Decorate a little. A few lights and posters make the cinderblock box feel less like a prison
cell and it genuinely helps homesickness the first month.
