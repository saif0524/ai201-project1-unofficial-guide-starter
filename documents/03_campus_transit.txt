Arlington is famous for having no city-wide public bus system, which surprises a lot of new
students. The good news is you can live at UTA without a car if you plan around it.

CAMPUS SHUTTLE
The Maverick Shuttle (the "Mav Mover") runs several routes connecting the residence halls,
the main academic core, the Park South commuter lots, and nearby apartment complexes. It's
free with your student ID. Routes run frequently during the day on weekdays and less often at
night and on weekends, so check the live tracker app before you wait at a stop.

RIDESHARE
The city's on-demand rideshare service covers the area around campus for a low flat fare and
is popular for grocery runs and trips that the shuttle doesn't reach. Regular Uber/Lyft also
operate here.

BIKING AND WALKING
Campus itself is compact — you can walk end to end in about 15 minutes, so most students never
need wheels to get to class. Bringing a bike helps if you live in the apartments just south of
campus. There are bike racks outside most buildings; bring a good U-lock.

GETTING TO DALLAS / FORT WORTH
For trips out of the city, students use the regional rail and bus connections (you'll
transfer via Trinity Metro toward Fort Worth or catch connections toward Dallas). It takes
planning, so most students coordinate carpools for weekend trips home.

BOTTOM LINE
A car is convenient but not required for freshman year, especially if you live on campus. Most
upperclassmen say the parking and permit cost isn't worth it until you move off campus.
