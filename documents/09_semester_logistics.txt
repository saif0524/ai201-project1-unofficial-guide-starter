Most of the avoidable stress freshman year comes from missing a date or a setting in MyMav.
Here's the logistics layer nobody explains.

REGISTRATION
- Your enrollment appointment time controls when you can first register. Find it in MyMav under
  the enrollment section before registration week.
- Clear any holds early. Holds (advising, financial, immunization) block registration entirely.
- Use waitlists and the swap function rather than dropping a class outright.

ADD / DROP
- The first week or two is the add/drop window — classes can be added or dropped with no
  penalty and no W on your transcript. This is the window where waitlists move and seats open.
- After the census date, dropping a class gives you a W and may affect financial aid.

FINANCIAL AID & BILLING
- Watch the payment deadline. If your bill isn't paid or covered by aid by the deadline, your
  classes can be dropped for non-payment and you'd have to re-register into whatever's left.
- Set up a payment plan in MyMav if you can't pay the full balance at once.

WITHDRAWAL DATES
- There's a last day to drop a class with a W well before finals. Mark it. Riding out a class
  you're failing because you missed the drop deadline is a common, costly mistake.

GENERAL
- Check your UTA email daily. Official deadlines, hold notices, and professor announcements all
  go there, not to your personal inbox.
