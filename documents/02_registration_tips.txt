OP: Registration opens next week and three of the classes I need are already full. What do
people actually do?

comment:
Step one is your enrollment appointment time in MyMav. You can't register before it, so log
in the minute it opens — full classes turn over fast in the first hour as people reshuffle.

comment:
Join the waitlist in MyMav even if it looks long. Waitlists move a LOT during the first two
weeks because of add/drop. I got into a capped CSE class from #6 on the waitlist by the second
day of the semester.

comment:
If there's no waitlist or it's full too, email the professor BEFORE the semester starts. Be
short and polite: your name, major, why you need the class this semester (graduation,
prerequisite chain), and ask if they'll sign an add/override. Profs can issue an electronic
permit that lets you register past the cap.

comment:
Also just physically go to the first lecture. Seats open up when no-shows get dropped, and
professors are way more likely to add you in person than over email.

comment:
Swap, don't drop. Use the MyMav "swap" function so you only let go of your current seat if the
new enrollment succeeds. People drop a class hoping to grab another and end up with neither.

comment:
Advising tip: if a required class is full every semester, talk to your academic advisor.
Sometimes they hold reserved seats for students in the major or can point you to an equivalent
section that isn't full.
