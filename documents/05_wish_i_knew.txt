When I started at UT Arlington I made basically every freshman mistake. Here's what I'd tell
my younger self.

1. Don't buy the parking permit until you're sure you need it. I spent money on a garage permit
and lived in Arlington Hall the whole year. The shuttle and walking covered everything.

2. Office hours are free tutoring. Professors are paid to sit there and almost nobody shows up.
I went from a C to an A in calculus just by showing up weekly.

3. Use the waitlist and email professors for full classes. I delayed graduation a semester
because I assumed "full" meant impossible. It doesn't.

4. The MAC (Maverick Activities Center) is included in tuition. I paid for an off-campus gym
for a year before realizing this. Don't be me.

5. Ration your meal swipes. I treated them like they were unlimited in September and was eating
ramen by November.

6. Find your quiet study spot early. For me it was the upper floors of the Central Library. The
first floor is a social scene, not a study space.

7. Get involved with one org, not five. I overcommitted, burned out, and dropped all of them.
One club you actually care about beats five you flake on.

8. Don't take all your hardest classes in one semester. Balance a heavy course with something
lighter so one bad week doesn't sink your GPA.

9. Learn to navigate MyMav early — registration, financial aid, holds, everything lives there.
A surprise hold can block you from registering at your appointment time.

10. Ask upperclassmen which professors to take. Same course, totally different experience
depending on who teaches it.
