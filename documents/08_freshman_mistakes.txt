OP: Upperclassmen, what mistake do you see freshmen make over and over?

comment (top):
Buying a parking permit they don't need. If you live on campus, you walk or take the shuttle.
Every year freshmen drop a few hundred dollars on a permit and barely use it.

comment:
Not checking MyMav for holds. A library fine, an unpaid balance, or a missing immunization
record puts a hold on your account and you can't register until it's cleared — people find out
the morning of their registration appointment and lose their good time slot.

comment:
Skipping office hours. It's the single highest-value free resource and freshmen avoid it like
it's a punishment.

comment:
Overloading their schedule. 18 hours of all-STEM your first semester is a classic trap. Ease
in, learn how college pacing works, then load up.

comment:
Ignoring the free stuff in tuition — the MAC, tutoring center, counseling, career services.
You already paid for it.

comment:
Treating meal swipes like they're infinite in the first month. Hard same. Budget them.

comment:
Not making friends in their major early. The people in your intro classes are your future
study group, project partners, and job referrals. Don't be a stranger.
