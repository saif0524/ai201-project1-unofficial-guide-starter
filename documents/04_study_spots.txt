OP: The first floor of the library is way too loud. Where do you all go to actually focus?

comment:
The Central Library gets quieter the higher you go. The lower floors are social/group-work
chaos, but the upper floors are designated quiet zones — people will shush you. Top floor is
the quietest. Bring a sweater, it's cold up there.

comment:
The Architecture building (CAPPA) has these big studio spaces and they're dead quiet at night.
Technically for arch students but nobody bothers you if you're just studying.

comment:
Honestly the grad study rooms you can reserve online through the library website are clutch
for group projects. Book one ahead, they fill up during finals.

comment:
For solo grinding I go to the Science and Engineering Library — smaller, less crowded than
Central, and the engineering crowd is there to work, not socialize.

comment:
Unpopular pick: the second floor of the University Center has quiet corners between the dining
areas in the late afternoon. Coffee is right there too.

comment:
During finals the Central Library extends hours and it becomes a 24-hour zone. The energy is
either motivating or terrifying depending on how prepared you are.
