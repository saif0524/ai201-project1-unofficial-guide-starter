OP: Incoming freshman here. Drop your best UTA advice below, I'll start a list.

top_comment (412 pts):
Biggest one: do NOT buy a parking permit before you know your schedule. So many freshmen
buy the expensive garage permit and then realize they live on campus and never drive. If you
do commute, the Park South lots are way cheaper and the shuttle picks up there.

reply (188 pts):
Go to office hours even when you don't have a question. Professors remember the students who
show up. It also makes asking for a recommendation letter junior year way less awkward.

reply (155 pts):
Use MyMav to set up a waitlist if a class is full. Most freshmen don't realize you can join
the waitlist and you'll often get auto-enrolled during the first week as people drop. Also
just show up to the first day of a full class and email the professor — a lot of them will
sign an add form if there's room.

reply (97 pts):
Don't schedule 8am classes your first semester. You think you'll make it. You will not.

reply (84 pts):
The Maverick Activities Center (the MAC) is free with tuition. Freshmen who actually use it
adjust to college way better imo. Plus it's a good place to meet people that isn't a party.

reply (61 pts):
Eat at the Connection Cafe early in the semester when your meal swipes feel infinite, then
ration them. Everyone runs out of swipes by November.

reply (40 pts):
Learn the building abbreviations. ERB, NH, WH, PKH — your schedule lists them and nobody
tells you what they mean. There's a campus map in the MyMav app.
